Are you looking to work in insurance, finance, operations research, or engineering? Are you considering a career in education teaching mathematics? Whatever your professional aspirations, if you are looking to work in the field of math and science, we have the associate degree to get you on the right track.

Undergradute Program

A degree in mathematics, at any level, shows employers that you are not afraid of hard work. It shows commitment, intelligence, and problem-solving skills necessary for a wide variety of career paths. If your career path is still unclear, an associate degree in STEM mathematics provides the foundational credentials and training you need for success in any career you choose.


What you will learn!

  • Learn how to solve problems using appropriate mathematical, technological, and scientific expertise.
  • Learn how to use technology to solve mathematical problems.
  • Study how to identify modern perspectives and appreciate events with informed insight.

Graduate Program

Pure mathematics graduate programs are theoretical. Theoretical mathematicians might identify, research and resolve unexplained issues in math. Students of pure math could study abstract concepts and formulas to prove or disprove theories, and solve math-related problems to boost knowledge about the field. Areas of pure mathematics generally draw from the topics that follow.

  • Algebra
  • Geometry and Topology
  • Calculus and Analysis
  • Combinatrics
  • Number Theory
